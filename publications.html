Research & Publications

Empowering Children in Digital Literacy through Innovative Game‑Based Methodology

Presented at the virtual workshop HCI4SouthAsia during CHI 2023. This talk explored how game‑based learning can foster digital literacy skills among children in developing regions.

A Cognitive Behaviour Data Analysis on the Use of Social Media in Global South Context Focusing on Bangladesh

Published in Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio). This open‑access article, released on 14 March 2023, investigates factors contributing to the propagation of misinformation on social media platforms in Bangladesh. The study was co‑authored by Shashwata Sourav Roy Samya and colleagues and analyses survey data using statistical techniques such as chi‑square tests and ordinal logistic regression to reveal that trust in the source, time spent online and content type influence the spread of fake news.

An open dataset associated with this work is available on GitHub.

Why Do We Share Fake News? A Cognitive Behaviour Data Analysis on the Use of Social Media in Global South Context

Abstract accepted at the Human–Computer Interaction International (HCII) 2022 conference. The abstract summarises our early findings on why individuals share fake news on social media and served as a precursor to the later journal article.
